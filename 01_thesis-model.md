# 01_thesis-model.md  
Thesis-Narrative OS — Thesis Model Specification

## 1. Definition of the Thesis

The Thesis is the **central value function** of the narrative.  
It defines the directional vector that structures all internal computations within the OS, including:

- value vector updates  
- emotional dynamics  
- subjective expression  
- event interpretation  
- narrative trajectory  

A Thesis is not a theme, motif, or message in the traditional literary sense.  
Within this OS, it is a **computable directional construct** that provides coherence and orientation to the entire value‑based system.

The Thesis has the following properties:

- **Directionality**  
  It specifies a target direction in the value‑dimension space.

- **Consistency**  
  It remains the central axis throughout the narrative.

- **Updatability**  
  Characters can converge toward, diverge from, or oscillate around it.

- **Evaluative Function**  
  It provides the basis for interpreting events, emotions, and subjective states.

---

## 2. Functional Role of the Thesis

### 2.1 Determining the Narrative Trajectory  
The Thesis defines the overall trajectory of the narrative.  
A character’s value vector moves in relation to the Thesis, producing:

- convergence  
- divergence  
- oscillation  

These movements form the narrative’s structural path.

### 2.2 Generating Conflict  
Conflict arises from the **vector difference** between:

- the Thesis  
- the character’s current value vector  

This difference drives tension, change, and emotional dynamics.

### 2.3 Guiding Event Updates  
Events are defined as **value‑vector update points**.  
The direction and magnitude of these updates are interpreted relative to the Thesis.

### 2.4 Influencing Emotion Dynamics  
The meaning of ΔValueVector (value fluctuation) depends on the Thesis.  
The Thesis determines whether a fluctuation represents:

- alignment  
- deviation  
- ambiguity  

This affects emotional strength and purity.

---

## 3. Structural Types of Theses

This classification describes **how a Thesis functions within the OS**,  
not how a creator should “come up with” one.

### 3.1 Question Type  
A central question defines the narrative’s direction.  
Characters move through exploratory trajectories.

Examples:  
- What is freedom?  
- Who determines what is right?

### 3.2 Assertion Type  
A clear claim defines the narrative’s direction.  
Characters move through trajectories of proof or refutation.

Examples:  
- Loneliness is not inherently harmful.  
- Justice resides in individual choice.

### 3.3 Directional Type  
An abstract value direction defines the narrative.  
Characters converge toward or deviate from this direction.

Examples:  
- Toward autonomy  
- Toward harmony

---

## 4. Relationship Between Thesis and Value Dimensions

The Thesis is represented as a **directional vector** in the value‑dimension space.  
This enables:

- distance calculations  
- update‑direction determination  
- emotional interpretation  
- orientation of subjective expression  

The Thesis acts as the **reference point** for all value‑based computations.

---

## 5. Relationship Between Thesis and Events

Events are moments when the value vector updates.  
The Thesis determines how these updates are interpreted:

- movement toward the Thesis  
- movement away from the Thesis  
- oscillation around the Thesis  

These directional updates form the narrative trajectory.

---

## 6. Relationship Between Thesis and Emotion Dynamics

Emotion Dynamics is defined as:



\[
Emotion = f(\Delta ValueVector \times EventIntensity)
\]



The meaning of ΔValueVector depends on the Thesis:

- fluctuation toward the Thesis  
- fluctuation away from the Thesis  
- fluctuation with unclear direction  

These distinctions influence emotional strength and purity.

---

## 7. Structural Requirements for a Thesis

A valid Thesis must satisfy the following structural conditions:

- **Directional**  
  Representable as a vector in value‑dimension space.

- **Consistent**  
  Functions as a stable axis throughout the narrative.

- **Appropriately Abstract**  
  High enough abstraction to allow meaningful value‑vector updates.

- **Updatable**  
  Supports character change and narrative progression.

- **Aligned with Value Dimensions**  
  Must be interpretable within the value‑dimension framework.

---

## 8. Out‑of‑Scope (Non‑Structural Elements)

This chapter defines **only the structural aspects** of the Thesis.  
The following are intentionally excluded:

- how to invent a Thesis  
- how to choose a Thesis  
- how to evaluate a Thesis  
- personal cognitive processes behind Thesis formation  
- individual value systems or subjective criteria  
- any internal OS specific to the creator  

These belong to the user’s internal model and are not part of the OS specification.
