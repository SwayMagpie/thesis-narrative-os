# 02_value-vector.md  
Thesis-Narrative OS — Value Vector Specification

## 1. Definition of the Value Vector

A Value Vector represents the **internal state of a character** within the value‑dimension space.  
Each dimension corresponds to a Value Dimension, and the vector defines:

- how the character moves in relation to the Thesis  
- how the character updates during events  
- how emotional dynamics are generated  
- how subjective expression emerges  

In this OS, a “character” is structurally defined as a **Value Vector and its update behavior**.

---

## 2. Fundamental Principle of Character Placement

Once the Thesis is defined, character placement becomes a matter of:

**“How does each character’s Value Vector move in relation to the Thesis?”**

From an OS perspective, all characters fall into two structural categories:

- **Those who shake the Thesis (holders of an answer)**  
- **Those who are shaken by the Thesis (non‑holders of an answer)**

This classification is not about personality or narrative role.  
It is a **structural classification of value‑vector behavior**.

---

## 3. Characters Who Shake the Thesis  
(Characters who *hold* their own “answer” to the Thesis)

These characters possess a clear stance toward the Thesis.  
Their Value Vectors have defined directionality relative to the Thesis.

### 3.1 Affirmers / Embodiers of the Thesis  
- Their vectors align with the Thesis  
- They reinforce the Thesis’s direction  
- They form the “convergence side” of the narrative trajectory

### 3.2 Opposers of the Thesis  
- Their vectors point against the Thesis  
- They generate structural conflict  
- They form the “counter‑directional side” of the trajectory

These characters create **the amplitude of oscillation** around the Thesis.

---

## 4. Characters Who Are Shaken by the Thesis  
(Characters who *do not hold* their own “answer”)

These characters lack a defined stance toward the Thesis.  
Their Value Vectors are undetermined or directionally ambiguous.

Characteristics:

- They oscillate in response to the Thesis  
- Their vectors undergo large updates during events  
- They often become the structural center of change

From an OS standpoint, they are the **primary source of ΔValueVector**  
and thus central to emotional and narrative dynamics.

---

## 5. Requirements for Character Count

Thesis-Narrative OS does **not** require multiple characters.  
The system is structurally independent of cast size.

In the extreme case:

- **One character alone**,  
- with all other influences represented as **events**,  

is sufficient for a complete narrative.

Reason:

- value‑vector updates  
- distance changes relative to the Thesis  
- emotional generation  
- subjective expression  
- world consistency  

These processes occur entirely within the OS,  
so narrative formation is fundamentally a **value‑vector update problem**,  
not a cast‑size problem.

---

## 6. Out‑of‑Scope (Non‑Structural Elements)

This chapter defines **only the structural aspects** of Value Vectors.  
The following are intentionally excluded:

- how to create character personalities  
- how to design backstories  
- how characters develop emotions  
- how characters form beliefs  
- how to invent a character’s “answer”  
- any creative techniques for character writing  

These depend on the user’s internal model  
and are not part of the OS specification.
